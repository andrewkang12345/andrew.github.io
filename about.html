:art: Welcome to my portfolio! I am Andrew Kang, an aspiring researcher from Rice University, dedicated to advancing AI-driven Human-Computer Interaction and interactive systems.

With a Bachelor's degree in computer science and a passion for interdisciplinary research, my work aims to push the boundaries of human-AI collaboration. Please explore my journey, projects, and achievements through the sections in the top right corner. I envision a future where immersive technology not only replicates reality but deeply influences how we perceive and respond to it. Inspired by concepts like TeleAbsence and projects like Memoro, I aim to integrate brain-computer interfaces and non-invasive brain stimulation to enhance hyperrealism. By inducing selective distraction or emphasizing key details during low-focus states, we can create tools that improve memory retention and emotional immersion. In training environments, VR can simulate real-world stress by introducing hyperrealistic noise, conditioning users to perform under pressure. My goal is to push the boundaries of human-computer interaction, merging neuroscience and immersive tech to redefine memory, training, and emotional connections.
